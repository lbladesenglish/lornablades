My Story

I was originally on the path to a career in marketing in London: a field that combined my love of creativity and communication. But during a backpacking trip to Canada, everything shifted. I met people from around the world and became fascinated by how language shapes connection, confidence, and opportunity.

That experience redirected my career. I moved to Spain and began teaching English, initially working in a corporate environment. Supporting professionals felt like a natural extension of my interest in business communication and cultural nuance.

Over the following years, I taught in Canada, New Zealand, and then back to Spain, developing a deep appreciation for how culture influences the way we communicate and collaborate. When the world moved online during the pandemic, I discovered my niche: working one-to-one with international professionals to help them communicate clearly, confidently, and authentically in high-stakes global environments.
